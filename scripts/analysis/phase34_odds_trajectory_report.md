# Phase 34 — First Look at the Odds-Trajectory Data

**Scope:** descriptive only. 9 days of `odds_snapshots` (2026-07-02 to 2026-07-11) supports
structure — how odds move, when books post, who moves first — not edge or profitability
claims. No ROI numbers, no pre-registered bars in this phase; those come later, on months
of data, if the structure below warrants them.

**Method:** read-only analysis of `odds_snapshots` + `fixtures` + `prediction_records`,
`scripts/analysis/phase34_odds_trajectory.py`. Zero API calls. All numbers below are
pulled directly from `phase34_results.json`, generated by that script against the live DB
at the time of writing. Every table states its own `n`.

---

## 1. Data Quality

**Volume.** 190,923 snapshot rows across 1,526 fixtures (3 with only 1 snapshot; 1,523
with ≥2). Mean 126.6 snapshots/fixture, median 115, but the distribution is wide (p10=27,
p90=229, max=665) — driven by how long a fixture sat in the 7-day capture window before
kickoff, not a capture problem.

**Qualifying fixtures** (settled, with ≥1 snapshot >6h before kickoff AND ≥1 snapshot ≤6h
before kickoff — matches the scheduler's own daily/hourly phase boundary): **915**, out of
1,087 settled fixtures with ≥2 snapshots. 169 settled fixtures have a near-kickoff touch
but no early one (short-notice entries into the 7-day window); only 3 have early-but-no-near
(a near-kickoff touch is virtually never missed — see bookmaker presence below). All Task 2/3
numbers below use these 915 fixtures.

**Capture spacing vs. design:**

| Phase | Design | Actual (median) | Actual (p25–p75) | n gaps |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Daily (>6h to kickoff) | ~24h | 20.45h | 11.5h – 20.5h | 54,383 |
| Hourly (≤6h to kickoff) | ~1h | 1.49h | 1.0h – 1.5h | 101,877 |

Both phases run close to design. The daily-phase median (20.45h) sits right at
`DAILY_PHASE_STALE_HOURS=20.0` — expected, since a fixture becomes a capture candidate the
moment it crosses that staleness threshold. The hourly phase's median of 1.49h vs. a 1h
target reflects the 30-min cron cadence plus per-run candidate ordering, not a systemic lag.

**Bookmaker presence over the fixture lifecycle** (% of fixtures with any snapshot in the
bucket that have a Pinnacle snapshot specifically, vs. 1xBet — the most complete soft
book, chosen because it's the modal non-Pinnacle bookmaker by row count):

| Hours to kickoff | n fixtures w/ any snapshot | Pinnacle % | 1xBet % |
|---|---|---|---|
| 0–1h | 791 | 87.7% | 95.1% |
| 1–2h | 1,016 | 88.3% | 96.5% |
| 2–6h | 1,287 | 88.2% | 98.0% |
| 6–24h | 1,048 | **43.9%** | 97.5% |
| 24–72h | 704 | 53.3% | 94.3% |
| 72–168h | 243 | 63.0% | 95.5% |

**This replicates the Phase 11b pattern cleanly.** 1xBet (and the other soft books, not
shown) are present on ~94–98% of fixtures at every horizon — they post early and stay
present. Pinnacle sits at 44–63% beyond 6h out and jumps to ~88% inside the final 6 hours.
Phase 11b's estimate that "~20% of apparent Pinnacle absences are early-fetch timing
artifacts, not genuine absences" is the right shape of story confirmed directly here:
Pinnacle simply hasn't posted yet for a large fraction of fixtures more than 6h from
kickoff, and shows up close to kickoff for the large majority of them.

**Structural gap, not a timing effect: Pinnacle has zero BTTS coverage.** Checked two
ways — 0 of 190,923 `odds_snapshots` rows, and separately 0 of 6,777 Pinnacle rows in the
unrelated `fixture_odds` table have `odd_btts_yes` populated. This isn't a capture-cadence
artifact (h2h: 6,257 Pinnacle rows; ou25: 5,567); the provider does not list a Pinnacle BTTS
price in this pipeline at all. Every BTTS analysis below that needs a sharp reference uses
1xBet instead, labeled explicitly. This also means Track B's Pinnacle-gated EV overlay is
structurally inert for BTTS — worth a look outside this phase's scope, since it wasn't
previously documented.

**July 2–8 scheduler-bug window — corrected.** The first version of this section parsed 412
runs of `odds_trajectory_scheduler.py` from `/var/log/bootball/odds_trajectory_scheduler.log`
and concluded near-kickoff was never starved. That parse only matched the log's *current*
print format, which starts at **2026-07-02 17:30 UTC** — 11 earlier runs (12:00–17:00 UTC
that day) use an older format the regex silently dropped instead of flagging as unparsed,
and that earlier window is exactly where the real incident lives. Reading those 11 runs
directly: **near-kickoff candidates were touched zero times for three consecutive 30-min
cycles — 16:00, 16:30, and 17:00 UTC on 2026-07-02** (20, 7, and 12 candidates respectively,
all touched 0) — because the scheduler's own daily budget (`collection_daily_cap`,
15,000 calls) hit exhaustion at 15:32 UTC that day (`Scheduler spend today: 15000 / 15000`),
and in this pre-fix code near-kickoff shared that same unified budget with the daily phase —
no separate accounting existed yet. The next run, 17:30 UTC, shows the fix already live:
`Near-kickoff candidates: 1 (touched 1, uncapped)` while daily-phase candidates stayed at
0 touched — near-kickoff had just been exempted from the shared cap entirely. So: **Phase 25's
"~90 minutes of near-kickoff starvation" is confirmed, precisely dated to 16:00–17:30 UTC on
2026-07-02**, and it is a real, if brief, one-time incident, not an ongoing risk — the fix
landed the same day and has held for all 412 runs since (`near_touched == near_candidates`
in every one). What the corrected parse also confirms from the post-fix window: `skipped_far_budget`
spiked to 14,266 on 2026-07-02 alone (vs. 600–1,300/day every day after) — a separate,
lower-severity cold-start effect (every fixture in the 7-day window was simultaneously "due"
on the very first run) that the daily-phase cap + `MAX_FAR_TOUCHES_PER_RUN=400` throttled
across the day's runs, exactly as designed. 290 of the 915 qualifying fixtures (32%) have
their first-ever touch on 2026-07-02, meaning their "open" reading may be later — and any
true early movement smaller — than a fixture captured under normal conditions. None of the
915 qualifying fixtures' *near-kickoff* touches fall inside the 16:00–17:30 starvation
window itself (that window produced zero snapshot rows by construction), so the starvation
incident cannot have corrupted any close-side reading used below — only the cold-start
effect's late-open risk, addressed next.

**Exclusion decision:** No blanket exclusion. Reasoning: (1) the near-kickoff/close side of
every pair is unaffected — the July-2 effect can only make "open" late, which biases
magnitude *conservatively downward*, not upward; (2) directly checked whether it distorts
the one place bias direction matters most — Task 2's timing split (below) — by rerunning it
on the 625 qualifying fixtures whose first touch was *not* on 2026-07-02: the final-6h
movement share moves from 35–39% (full sample) to 33.9–36.9% (clean subset) — a ~1–2pp
shift, not a material one. The July-2 cohort is flagged here for transparency but not
excluded from any table below.

## 2. Movement Structure

### 2.1–2.2 Magnitude and direction (Pinnacle, sharp reference; h2h/ou25)

Open (>6h) → close (≤6h) implied-probability change, per side:

| Market | Side | n | Mean \|Δ\| (pp) | Median \|Δ\| (pp) | p75 \|Δ\| (pp) | Mean signed Δ (pp) |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| h2h | home | 439 | 2.13 | 1.32 | 3.00 | +0.20 |
| h2h | draw | 439 | 0.99 | 0.69 | 1.34 | −0.38 |
| h2h | away | 439 | 1.89 | 1.26 | 2.67 | −0.43 |
| ou25 | over | 392 | 1.70 | 1.03 | 2.42 | +0.45 |
| ou25 | under | 392 | 1.72 | 1.10 | 2.45 | −0.80 |

**Lines move, but modestly, in this (mostly non-elite) league mix.** Typical Pinnacle
movement is ~1–2pp; three-quarters of fixtures move less than 3pp on any given side. Soft
books move *more* than Pinnacle on the same fixtures — e.g. 1xBet's h2h-home median is
2.31pp (n=879) vs. Pinnacle's 1.32pp — consistent with Pinnacle's reputation for tighter,
more stable pricing.

**Favorite direction (highest-opening-implied-prob side, all books/markets pooled):**

| Market | n | % shortened | % drifted | % flat | Mean signed Δ (pp) |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| h2h | 7,660 | 38.7% | 40.7% | 20.6% | +0.02 |
| ou25 | 5,632 | 39.6% | 31.6% | 28.8% | +0.30 |
| btts | 5,106 | 34.8% | 33.3% | 32.0% | +0.08 |

No systematic favorite-shortening or -drifting bias — all three markets sit close to a
coin flip, consistent with an efficient-ish market and/or a sample too early to detect a
small directional bias if one exists.

**The fdco-era "Overs drift toward Unders" finding does not replicate here.** Tracking
the Over side specifically (n=5,632, Pinnacle+soft pooled): 40.3% shorten vs. 31.4% drift,
mean signed Δ = **+0.47pp** — Over probability rises slightly on average, the *opposite*
direction from the historical (elite-league, multi-season fdco) finding of a systematic
over-drift-out. This is a genuinely different result, not noise around zero, though 9 days
is a thin base for a directional claim either way — flagged as "does not replicate in this
league mix / this short a window," not as a reversal of the original finding.

### 2.3 Timing — where does the movement happen?

Pinnacle only, split into the open→6h leg and the 6h→close leg:

| Market | Side | n | Mean \|move\| open→6h (pp) | Mean \|move\| 6h→close (pp) | % of total move in final 6h |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| h2h | home | 439 | 1.63 | 0.95 | 36.8% |
| h2h | draw | 439 | 0.80 | 0.43 | 35.1% |
| h2h | away | 439 | 1.43 | 0.86 | 37.5% |
| ou25 | over | 392 | 1.33 | 0.87 | 39.6% |
| ou25 | under | 392 | 1.33 | 0.83 | 38.4% |

**Movement is meaningfully concentrated in the final 6 hours.** ~35–39% of total absolute
movement happens in that window even though it's a small fraction of a fixture's total time
in the capture pool (which can span up to 7 days) — the final-6h "rate" of movement per
hour is far higher than the daily-phase rate. This means our ~1h cadence inside 6h *should*
be fine-grained enough to see real intra-window structure if any exists; the daily (~20h
gap) cadence outside 6h cannot resolve anything finer than day-level moves.

### 2.4 Book-vs-book: who moves first?

**A design fact found while building this analysis, worth stating plainly:** one API
response returns every bookmaker's price for a fixture at once. Pinnacle and every soft
book share the *identical* `captured_at` timestamp (down to the microsecond) within a
touch — verified directly against the raw rows. There is no continuous-time gap between
books to measure; "when Pinnacle moves, does book X follow, and after how long" can only
be answered in units of **touches** (the capture cadence itself), not minutes. An earlier
version of this analysis computed a timestamp-based "delay" between books and it was
~always zero by construction — a methodology bug, not a finding; discarded.

**Move threshold:** touch-to-touch implied-probability changes are exactly zero ~72% of
the time (no reprice that cycle); among nonzero changes the median magnitude is ~1.5pp. A
move is counted at ≥1.0pp — just under that median, keeping most genuine reprices while
dropping the smallest residual noise.

Per (market, side, soft book) — median across all 61 (market, side, book) cells, each with
≥31 Pinnacle move events (most cells have 400–600+; Superbet's cells are the thinnest):

| | Median across cells |
|---|---|
| % of Pinnacle moves where the soft book reprices at the *same touch* | 57.2% |
| ...in the *same direction* | 56.1% |
| % where the soft book only catches up one touch later | ~2–3% (see full table) |
| % where the soft book doesn't follow within 1 touch at all | 38.6% |

**Most books move together, at our resolution, when the reprice is large enough to see —
less so for the draw and for the thinner books.** Same-touch co-movement across the 51
(market, side, book) cells for the 9 highest-volume soft books (1xBet, Marathonbet, Bet365,
Betfair, William Hill, Betano, Dafabet, 10Bet, BetVictor) ranges **28.6–78.2%**, and it isn't
random which end a cell falls on: the home/away sides for the biggest books (1xBet,
Marathonbet, Betfair, William Hill, Bet365) cluster at the high end, 60–78%, while the
smaller-magnitude **draw** side and **SBO** specifically sit consistently lower, 28.6–43%
(draw moves are roughly half the size of home/away moves per the magnitude table above, so
fewer of them clear the 1pp move threshold at the exact same touch by chance). Two books are
extreme outliers regardless of side: **Unibet** (16.6–24.9% same-touch, 70.4–82.1%
never-follows) and, more noisily given a much smaller n (31–73 move events per cell vs.
400–600+ for the majority books), **Superbet** (17.8–47.3% same-touch, 52.7–82.2%
never-follows). Unibet's own open→close magnitude table corroborates its pattern
independently — its h2h-home *median* Δ is **0.0pp** (n=500, mean 1.66pp), meaning its price
literally doesn't move between open and close for the majority of fixtures. This reads as
Unibet (and likely Superbet) repricing on a much sparser schedule than the other soft books
in this feed, not as evidence they lead or lag Pinnacle in a timing sense. In all cases, the
"delayed, catch-up move exactly one touch later" pathway stays rare (0–6.5% per cell) —
disagreement with Pinnacle mostly resolves to "moved together" or "didn't move within a
touch," almost never to a clean one-step lag.

**Bottom line on "who moves first":** at ~hourly resolution, we cannot see meaningful
lead/lag between Pinnacle and the majority of soft books — they appear to move together,
either because the underlying signal (results, injury news, sharp money) reaches all of
them within the same hour, or because our cadence is too coarse to catch a true sub-hour
sequence. We cannot distinguish those two explanations with this data.

## 3. Our Predictions vs. the Movement (toward/away-from-us)

Reference bookmaker per market: **Pinnacle** for h2h/ou25, **1xBet** for btts (Pinnacle has
no BTTS price at all — see Data Quality above). For each qualifying, Pinnacle/1xBet-covered
fixture, compared `|open_implied − our_prob|` vs. `|close_implied − our_prob|` for our
model's predicted side (raw `our_prob` — see note below on why not `served_prob`).

| Market | n | % toward us | % away from us | % unchanged | Mean dist open (pp) | Mean dist close (pp) |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| h2h | 433 | 40.0% | 50.1% | 9.9% | 17.17 | 17.44 |
| ou25 | 377 | 39.5% | 46.9% | 13.5% | 15.68 | 15.74 |
| btts | 824 | 42.2% | 44.9% | 12.9% | 23.86 | 23.96 |
| **Overall** | **1,634** | **41.0%** | **46.8%** | **12.2%** | **20.20** | **20.34** |

**The market does not move toward us on net — if anything, slightly away.** Across all
three markets, "away from us" outpaces "toward us" by 5–10pp, and the mean distance from
our probability widens slightly by close in every market (never narrows). This is a small
effect on a 9-day sample and should not be read as "the market ignores our model" or any
directional edge claim — it's a plain description of where the numbers land.

**"Model high" cut** (self-computed split: top quartile of opening disagreement,
`|our_prob − open_implied| ≥ 29.33pp`, vs. the bottom three quartiles):

| Cut | n | % toward us | % away from us | Mean dist open (pp) | Mean dist close (pp) |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Strong disagreement (≥p75) | 409 | 42.1% | 47.2% | 37.70 | 37.64 |
| Weak disagreement (<p75) | 1,225 | 40.7% | 46.6% | 14.36 | 14.56 |

No meaningful difference in toward/away rate between strong- and weak-disagreement cases
(42.1% vs. 40.7% toward). The strong-disagreement cut's mean distance is essentially flat
(37.70→37.64) while the weak cut widens slightly (14.36→14.56) — a marginal, not
statistically anchored, difference. **No evidence either that "the market comes to us" when
we disagree strongly, or that we're simply wrong and get corrected** — the data doesn't
distinguish those stories at this n.

**Note on `served_prob`:** Phase 33b just added a frozen, calibration-adjusted
`served_prob` column to `prediction_records` (persisted at kickoff, going forward only).
At the time of this analysis it's populated on ~50 rows — nowhere near enough to redo this
section against "what we actually served" instead of raw `our_prob`. This section used raw
`our_prob`, which — because `save_predictions()` upserts a single row per fixture/market and
stops updating once a fixture leaves `NS` — approximates *our last view before kickoff*,
not our opening view. Worth rerunning against genuine `served_prob` once enough
post-Rider-4 fixtures settle (see Phase 35 trigger below).

## 4. Synthesis and the Phase 35 Trigger

**What structure exists, in one paragraph:** Odds move in this league mix, but modestly —
typically 1–2pp on the sharp reference between open and close, concentrated
disproportionately (~35–39% of total movement) in the final 6 hours despite that being a
small fraction of total capture time. Pinnacle posts late (44–63% presence beyond 6h out,
jumping to ~88% inside 6h) exactly as Phase 11b found originally. Most soft books reprice in
lockstep with Pinnacle at our hourly resolution — we cannot see who moves first because
either nobody leads by more than an hour, or our cadence can't catch it if they do. The
market does not systematically move toward our predictions; it moves away marginally more
often than toward, with no detectable difference when we disagree strongly with the
opening line vs. weakly. The one clear directional finding that breaks from prior work — the
Over side trending to *shorten* rather than *drift* here — is presented as a genuine,
unexplained departure from the older fdco-era result, not a replication.

**What 9 days cannot say:** Whether any of these patterns are stable (9 days is one week of
football, dominated by whatever leagues happen to be in-season right now); whether the
"movement moves away from us" finding reflects anything about our model's calibration or is
just sampling noise around a true zero; whether Unibet/Superbet's sparse repricing is
permanent behavior or a temporary feed issue; whether soft-book lead/lag exists below our
1-hour resolution floor. None of this supports an ROI or edge claim, and none was computed.

**Pre-registered Phase 35 trigger — set now, before more data arrives:**

1. **Timing/lead-lag:** A sub-hourly-resolution study (tighter capture cadence in the final
   1–2h, or an out-of-band spot-check) becomes warranted **only if** a future run of this
   same hourly-cadence analysis, on ≥3,000 qualifying fixtures (roughly 3x today's sample),
   still shows the same-touch co-movement rate holding above ~55% median across the majority
   of soft books — i.e., the "we can't resolve lead/lag" finding is itself stable, not just
   an artifact of today's small n. If instead the same-touch rate *drops* substantially with
   more data (regression to a lower, noisier baseline), that says our current finding was
   sampling luck and a tighter-cadence study is premature.
2. **CLV/edge testing:** Warranted only once (a) the qualifying-fixture sample reaches
   ≥3,000 (chronological, spanning multiple weeks/leagues, not just this window's schedule),
   AND (b) `served_prob` has enough settled coverage (≥500 settled rows with a frozen
   `served_prob`, per Phase 33b) to redo Section 3 against what we actually served instead of
   raw `our_prob`. Until both hold, any ROI number computed from this pipeline would be
   describing either too few fixtures or the wrong probability.
3. **If the structure is null instead** (movement magnitude stays this small, timing
   concentration doesn't hold up, no book ever separates from the pack) — that is itself an
   answer: it would mean the Track B data doesn't reward more collection investment sooner
   than the model/calibration work Track A already benefits from, and effort should stay on
   Track A (calibration, coverage, model quality) rather than building an edge-testing
   pipeline on odds trajectories.

No code changed. No numbers here should be read as, or used to justify, a betting decision.
